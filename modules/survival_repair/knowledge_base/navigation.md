# Navigation Without GPS
## Using Sun for Direction
1. Morning - sun rises roughly east
2. Noon - sun is due south (northern hemisphere) or north (southern)
3. Afternoon - sun sets roughly west
4. Shadow stick method - place stick, mark shadow tip, wait 15min, mark again - line points east-west

## Using Stars
1. North Star (Polaris) - find Big Dipper, follow pointer stars 5x their distance
2. Southern Cross - points roughly south in southern hemisphere
3. Moon - if crescent moon, line between tips points south (N hemisphere)

## Reading Landscape
1. Rivers flow downhill to sea - follow for civilization
2. Roads and powerlines lead to settlements
3. Aircraft contrails show direction of airports
4. Moss grows on north side of trees (N hemisphere) - not always reliable

## Offline Maps Tips
1. Download maps before leaving signal area
2. Screenshot key sections as backup
3. Note GPS coordinates of camp each night
4. Carry paper map of region as ultimate backup
5. Mark waypoints every 50km when in remote areas
