# Fire and Heat Guide
## Starting Fire Without Lighter
1. Bow drill method - requires dry wood, takes 10-20 minutes practice
2. Flint and steel - carry as backup in kit
3. Battery + steel wool - touch wool to both terminals
4. Fresnel lens or glasses - focus sunlight on dark tinder

## Fire in Wet Conditions
1. Find dry wood under fallen logs or inside dead standing trees
2. Use birch bark - contains oils that burn when wet
3. Build platform first - keep fire off wet ground
4. Start small, protect flame from wind with your body

## Vehicle Heating Without Power
1. Insulate windows with foam mats or sleeping bags
2. Two people generate significant heat in small van
3. Hot water bottle - boil water, fill bottle, place in sleeping bag
4. Chemical hand warmers last 8-12 hours
5. Never run engine in closed space - carbon monoxide risk

## Cooking Without Gas
1. Hobo stove - punch holes in tin can, burn small sticks
2. Solar cooker - reflective material focuses sun on pot
3. Retained heat cooking - boil then wrap pot in blankets for 2h
